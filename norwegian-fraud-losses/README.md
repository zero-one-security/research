# Norwegian social-engineering losses

**NOK 667 million was lost to social engineering across the Norwegian financial
system in 2024: NOK 360 million in the first half and NOK 307 million in the
second, the sum of two published Finanstilsynet half-year figures.**

This is the figure behind the "the loss is already counted" claim in the
magazine. It is a directly cited public figure, not an estimate, so this study is
a ledger: the two half-year numbers, their primary source, and the limits on what
the sum supports.

## The figures

| Period | Loss | Source |
|---|---|---|
| H1 2024 | NOK 360 million | Finanstilsynet, Svindelstatistikk første halvår 2024 |
| H2 2024 | NOK 307 million | Finanstilsynet, Svindelstatistikk andre halvår 2024 |
| **2024 total** | **NOK 667 million** | Sum of the two published half-year figures |

## Scope and caveats (read before quoting)

- **It is a national figure, not a firm-level one.** This is loss across the
  whole Norwegian financial system, reported by the financial regulator. It is
  not a statistic about any one firm, sector, or profession.
- **It is a sum of two published figures, not an estimate.** 360 + 307 = 667.
  Both halves are Finanstilsynet's own published fraud statistics.
- **It does not claim a single cause.** It does not follow that every loss began
  with a stolen password or a phished credential. "Social engineering" spans
  several deception techniques. The figure shows that deception-driven loss in
  Norway is already very large, not that one control would have prevented it.
- **It is a fraud figure, not a cyber-insurance figure.** This loss is generally
  routed to fraud cover, not core cyber insurance, so it is out of scope as a
  cyber-insurance-penetration argument. See the "considered and not used" note in
  [`../norwegian-cyber-insurance/data/sources.md`](../norwegian-cyber-insurance/data/sources.md).
