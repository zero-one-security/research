# Norwegian SMB cyber incidents

The incident cases and aggregate figures behind the "stakes" examples on the
site and in the outreach magazine, chosen for the audience we actually serve:
small Norwegian firms (5–50 people) holding sensitive client data.

This study **supersedes the example lineup** in `../norwegian-incident-costs`
(Stortinget / AKVA / Hydro), which were large, public, or industrial entities a
small firm does not recognise itself in.

## What we use, and the honest limits

- **Two named cases**, not big abstract numbers:
  - **Total Revisjon DA** (Moss revisjonsbyrå) — a firm exactly like the reader.
  - **Nordlo-dommen** — the legal principle that you cannot outsource the risk
    for your own data. The plaintiffs were *not* professional-services firms, so
    this case carries the principle, not relatability.
- **One aggregate anchor:** Mørketallsundersøkelsen 2024 — ~8 % of businesses hit
  in a year, and only 24 % of breaches reach the police (the reported floor).
- **Frequency claim is out.** Mørketall shows reported incidents *rise* with
  size (7 % at 5–19 ansatte vs 18 % at 100+). The defensible claims are
  preparedness, underreporting, and disproportionate consequence — never "small
  firms are hit most often."
